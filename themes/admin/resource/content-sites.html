Evernote Integration

Sandbox

The Evernote Sandbox allows you to test Noteworthy with a test Evernote account. This is useful if you'd like to see whether the software is a good match for what you want to accomplish, or if you just don't trust my software with your real Evernote account.

Production

Production refers to the real Evernote servers. By using the Production servers you will be able to share content from the notebooks you specify online.

Import Your Data

Once Evernote is Configured, you can import the data from your selected notebooks. This may take a little bit of time depending on the size and number of notes in your selected folders. Once the first import is complete, new records will be searched for every hour.

About Cron Jobs

Cron is the time-based job scheduler found on many servers. Cron enables us to schedule jobs (commands or shell scripts) to run periodically at certain times or dates. It is commonly used to automate system maintenance or administration and, in the case of Noteworthy, this includes connecting to various services online to look for new content.

Setting up a cron job is quite easy if you have shell access on your web server. Simply type:

crontab -e

And you're in. If this is the first time you've used crontab, the file will be empty. You can have the server check for updates every 15 minutes by typing the following:

*/15 * * * * /usr/bin/wget -q --timeout=600 "[HOMEURL]/cron/"

15 minutes should be a decent time interval between cron jobs, but keep in mind that Tweets will only be checked every five minutes and Evernote every half-hour. This is to ensure these services don't block or ban you from accessing their servers. Want to learn more about cron jobs? Wikipedia has a good article on them explaining how they work and how to use them.

About Comments

Noteworthy does not have local storage of comments. Instead, the free commenting service Disqus can allow visitors to leave feedback on your posts. You can sign up for a Disqus account here. The necessary code and resources will automagically appear on your site after entering your Disqus ID.
